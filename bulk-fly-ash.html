Transport of Bulk Fly Ash from Thermal Power Plants to Cement Factories

As cement plants require dry fly ash, earlier some thermal power plants arranged loading of fly ash directly from ESP hoppers into trucks. This practice resulted in severe air pollution due to fugitive dust emissions. Following the recommendations of the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) under Corporate Responsibility for Environmental Protection (CREP), thermal power plants initiated the construction of silos for intermediate storage of dry fly ash and implemented proper loading mechanisms.

Fly ash loaded from these silos is transported to cement plants using specially designed bulkers. However, fly ash transportation involves several challenges such as transportation distance, form of ash (dry or wet), user requirements, economic feasibility, surrounding environmental conditions and other site-specific factors.

Controlling dust emission during transportation is the primary concern, especially since fly ash movement covers larger areas and passes through multiple sensitive receptors. Fly ash is utilized in various applications including cement manufacturing, brick production, mine backfilling, road construction and filling of low-lying areas. Therefore, handling and transportation methods must be carefully selected based on end use.

To minimize fugitive dust emissions, appropriate transportation modes and precautionary measures are essential for different end users of fly ash or bottom ash.

Transportation Guidelines

User Type-wise Fly Ash Transportation

User Type 1

Cement / Asbestos Manufacturing

Specially designed road bulkers or mechanically designed covered trucks equipped with automatic loading and unloading systems using compressors or vacuum pumps mounted on tankers must be used.

User Type 2

Fly Ash Based Brick, Tiles & Blocks Manufacturing

  • Tankers / bulkers or mechanically designed covered trucks must be used.
  • Tractor trolleys with box-type top cover and hydraulic unloading systems shall be deployed for transportation of dry or wet fly ash while passing through inhabited areas.
  • For short distances, tractor trolleys covered with good quality HDPE tarpaulin may be permitted.
User Type 3

Road Construction & Filling of Low-Lying Areas

Tankers, bulkers or mechanically designed covered trucks must be used shown to prevent fugitive dust emissions during transportation.

User Type 4

Other Miscellaneous Purposes

Only tankers, bulkers or mechanically designed covered trucks are permitted for transportation.
